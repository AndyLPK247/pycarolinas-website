Registration

Please arrive on time to check in for the conference.

Lightning Talks

A "lightning talk" is a brief talk about an interesting topic. They can show how to use a Python library, demo a cool project, or share lessons learned. Lightning talks give ordinary attendees a chance to share extraordinary things!

At PyCarolinas, all talks will be lightning talks. Our lightning talks will be 5 to 10 minutes long. We encourage anyone who is interested to give a lightning talk, especially first-time speakers. If you are interested in giving a talk, please sign up!

Collab Clusters

A "collab cluster" is a space for a group to work together on a project. It could be a bunch of coders hacking together on an open source project. It could be a group discussion about a special interest. It could be someone leading an impromptu tutorial. It could also be someone offering help or guidance to others who are stuck on problems.

At PyCarolinas 2020, we want to use the space we have to foster organic collaboration. Feel free to start your own collab cluster on the day of the conference. If you'd like to plan ahead, please sign up so we can promote it for you!

Hallway Track

The "hallway track" refers to spontaneous encounters at the conference. They typically happen in spaces where people cross paths. We should have plenty of space for attendees to mix and mingle. Strike up conversations, share projects, and swap stickers!

Meals and Refreshments

Meals and refreshments are dependent upon sponsors. Right now, the only meal we intend to provide is a pizza lunch with soft drinks. We are open to providing morning coffee and possibly a dinner party, too. Please refer to the sponsors page and contact us if you can help find sponsors!
