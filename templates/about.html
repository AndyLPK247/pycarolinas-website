About PyCarolinas

PyCarolinas is the regional Python conference for the Carolinas. PyCarolinas is a non-profit conference run for the Python community, by the Python community. As organizers, we seek to promote learning and camaraderie around the Python programming language in a fun, safe, and diverse environment that is welcoming to all. Please help us do this by following our code of conduct.

Why the "Unconference"?

A traditional conference is a formal event with top-down organization. Organizers plan a program, invite speakers, and schedule events. An unconference, however, is participant-driven. Unconferences facilitate attendant-driven activities by providing open spaces and some guidance.

There are three primary reasons why we chose to make PyCarolinas 2020 an unconference:

  1. COVID-19 could force us to reschedule or cancel PyCarolinas 2020. We want to structure the conference to be as lean as possible so that changes will be less disruptive for all involved. Please read more about our pandemic preparedness on our COVID-19 page.
  2. The organizers have been very busy with non-PyCarolinas things. A traditional conference would be a lot more work than an unconference, and we just might not have time to pull it off.
  3. We want to make PyCarolinas distinct among regional Python conferences. Most other conferences follow a traditional format. That's awesome, and there is nothing wrong with that, but we want to try something different.

Automation Panda's PyCarolinas 2020 Update shares the full story. We do not know if we will hold unconferences for future conferences, but for 2020, we simply know that an unconference will be the best format given our circumstances.

PyCarolinas History

Calvin Spealman founded PyCarolinas in 2012 with the first (and so far only) conference at UNC Chapel Hill. The only other Python conference held in the Carolinas since then was PyData Carolinas 2016, hosted by IBM. Despite having several talented Pythonistas in both North and South Carolina, various factors prevented the return of either conference.

Starting in 2018, Pandy Knight had a dream to make a regional Python conference happen in the Carolinas. Pandy shared this idea with some friends at PyCon 2019 who strongly encouraged him to make it happen. Dustin Ingram then invited Pandy to give a call-to-action for a PyCarolinas 2020 conference on the big stage during the "parade of worldwide conferences." Immediately thereafter, Pandy held an open space for PyCarolinas to kick things off, and dozens of people attended. Pandy got in touch with Calvin after PyCon, and they became PyCarolinas co-chairs.

PyCarolinas 2020 will truly be a relaunch for the conference. We want to start small this year so that we can sustainably build momentum for years to come. PyCarolinas 2020 will happen in Raleigh, NC, but we are open to other locations throughout the Carolinas in the future.
